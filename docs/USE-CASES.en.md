# Ulurin: Concrete Use Cases

🌐 **Language:** English (this document) · [Bahasa Indonesia](USE-CASES.md)

> Every person, account, movement, and institution named here is a public example used to ground product design. They are **not claimed Ulurin partners or users**. Statistics are dated/sourced and may change.

## 1. Environmental movement: Pandawara Group

### Who they are and what they do

[Pandawara Group on TikTok](https://www.tiktok.com/@pandawaragroup) is a five-person youth movement from Bandung that documents river and beach clean-ups and mobilizes volunteers.

- Guinness recorded **12,137,832 TikTok followers on 29 March 2026**, the largest following for an environmental clean-up group. ([Guinness World Records](https://www.guinnessworldrecords.com/world-records/782570-most-followers-on-tiktok-for-an-environmental-clean-up-group))
- Forbes included them in **30 Under 30 Asia 2025** and reports the group's claim that more than **1.3 million kilograms of waste** had been removed with thousands of volunteers. ([Forbes](https://www.forbes.com/profile/pandawara-group/))
- A Waste4Change profile describes needs including truck rental, trash bags, equipment, and operating costs, along with a funding journey from personal money toward donations and sponsors. ([Waste4Change](https://waste4change.com/blog/pandawara-group-pemuda-inspiratif-penggiat-bebersih-sungai/))

### The business problem

A large following proves distribution and trust; it is not cash flow. A per-event CSR sponsor can fund one activity, but the movement must find another sponsor next month. When fieldwork depends on viral content or occasional sponsorship, capacity is difficult to plan.

### The Ulurin campaign

**Recurring campaign:** “Four Bandung river clean-ups in August.”

Before paying, a donor sees:

- number of activities and target locations;
- truck, fuel, protective equipment, tools, volunteer meals, and waste-handling budget;
- the 2% platform fee;
- the 0–5% Creator Reward and work explanation;
- the project/organization wallet receiving the operating allocation;
- required proof at the end of each activity and month.

Each cycle produces transaction receipts, invoices, location documentation, work results, and completion status. The Creator Reward remains separate from the project budget.

### Value created

- the movement can plan monthly capacity;
- followers can become recurring donors without waiting for a viral campaign;
- CSR sponsors receive activity-level reporting;
- creators earn disclosed income instead of hiding it inside project expenses;
- reputation grows from completed work, not views alone.

## 2. Road-repair volunteers

### Nanda / @relawan.jalan.rusak

[Nanda on TikTok](https://www.tiktok.com/@relawan.jalan.rusak) was reported to have patched roughly **79 potholes over two years** in Riau. One related video passed 800,000 views. The work used personal funds and follower support. ([Catatan Riau](https://catatanriau.com/news/detail/28114/viral-relawan-jalan-rusak-di-pekanbaru-ditegur-rw-saat-tambal-jalan))

### Suted / @suted.sukarno

[Suted on TikTok](https://www.tiktok.com/@suted.sukarno) is an online motorcycle-taxi driver in Demak who experiences dangerous roads through his daily work. He and the Maleng Gank community patched roads from Jalan Pemuda to the Demak terminal overnight; livestream viewers then contributed. ([Jateng News](https://www.jatengnews.id/2026/02/25/ojol-demak-tambal-jalan-berlubang-dini-hari-tuai-simpati-warganet/))

### The business problem

Livestream gifts and personal transfers connect goodwill with action, but do not give donors a budget, road scope, compensation split, purchase evidence, or consistent completion history. Support also depends on a viral moment.

### The Ulurin campaign

**Finite campaign:** “Repair 12 potholes on road X before date Y.”

- Beneficiary/project: a verified road-repair project wallet.
- Budget: asphalt/materials, tools, safety signs, transport, and waste removal.
- Creator Reward: survey time, neighborhood coordination, procurement, labor, and documentation.
- Completion evidence: geolocation, before/after media, material receipts, and local-community confirmation.

The campaign ends when the agreed scope is complete, not when the content stops trending.

## 3. Creators who bridge help to another person

[Ahwar / @mazz.ojol.sby](https://www.tiktok.com/@mazz.ojol.sby), a Surabaya gig driver, uses livestreams to help elderly street vendors reach buyers and donors. In one action he streamed for an extended period so viewers could buy out a vendor's stock or entrust charity to him. ([Detik](https://www.detik.com/jatim/berita/d-8470628/cerita-mas-ojol-surabaya-live-tiktok-24-jam-demi-bantu-dagangan-lansia))

**Ulurin use case:** after identity, need, and consent checks, the creator opens a campaign for one vendor. Donors see whether money will buy stock, provide business capital, cover healthcare, or meet another defined need; the vendor allocation is separated from the Creator Reward; and the outcome remains available after the livestream ends.

## 4. Homes and services that must operate every month

A recurring campaign is different from an emergency. An orphanage is not “finished” after one bag of rice; an elderly home still needs medicine next month; a free kitchen must cook again tomorrow.

### Orphanage / children's home

**Recurring needs:** meals, school, uniforms and books, hygiene, health, electricity, water, rent, and caregivers. [Panti Asuhan Indonesia](https://pantiasuhan.or.id/) shows food and education campaign categories. Both needs continue after one distribution.

**Evidence cycle:** monthly cost summary, redacted invoices, school or supplier payments, non-exploitative program updates, and confirmation from a verified operator.

**Guardrail:** a child's face, full name, school, documents, and location cannot become the price of receiving support.

### Elderly home

**Recurring needs:** meals, medicine, adult-care supplies, health checks, medical transport, housing, utilities, and carers. A [Dompet Dhuafa campaign](https://digital.dompetdhuafa.org/donasi/lansiapantijompo) documents food support for 30 elderly residents and distribution updates.

**Evidence cycle:** purchase evidence, service schedule, beneficiary count, institution confirmation, and next-cycle needs, without casually exposing personal health conditions.

### Free-meal kitchen

**Recurring needs:** rice, protein, vegetables, cooking gas, water, packaging, equipment, transport, rent, and kitchen labor. [Rumah Cinta Qur'an](https://rumahcintaquran.or.id/rumah-makan-gratis/) runs a free-meal program for low-income people, travelers, and anyone who needs food. Masjid Baitul Huda Antapani was also reported to provide hundreds of free portions daily with cross-community support. ([Arrahimedia](https://www.arrahimedia.or.id/2025/02/masjid-baitul-huda-antapani-sediakan.html))

**Ulurin campaign:** “Provide lunch for 30 days.” Donors see the portion target, cost categories, schedule, Creator Reward, and 2% platform fee. Updates report portions and ingredient usage; evidence does not need to expose recipient faces.

### Animal shelter

**Recurring needs:** feed, rent, veterinary care, sterilization, medicine, rescue transport, and cleaning. Reporting on Animal Defenders Indonesia described organizers covering veterinary costs personally when income was unstable. ([Komunita](https://komunita.id/2016/02/01/animal-defenders-indonesia-berjuang-demi-hewan-hewan-telantar/))

**Evidence cycle:** veterinary invoices, animal-level outcomes, treatment/adoption results, current occupancy, and feed purchases.

### Patient house and rehabilitation centre

**Recurring needs:** temporary housing, meals, hospital transport, medicine, therapy, support workers, and staff. Evidence must create accountability without broadcasting sensitive health information.

### Legal aid and independent research

**Recurring needs:** case intake, filing fees, travel, professional hours, data collection, and publication. Proof consists of reviewable milestones and deliverables, redacted to protect clients or research subjects.

## 5. How a recurring campaign works

1. The donor selects an amount and authorizes recurring payment through a familiar rupiah rail.
2. Before authorization, the donor sees the beneficiary/project allocation, Creator Reward, platform fee, and total per cycle.
3. Each month is a new transaction cycle; an off-chain scheduler submits payment under the authorization.
4. The beneficiary/project allocation becomes available under campaign rules.
5. That month's Creator Reward remains locked until cycle evidence is complete.
6. The beneficiary/institution and/or reviewer confirms the evidence state.
7. The donor receives an update and receipt, with the ability to continue, change, or stop support.
8. Creator and campaign profiles show cycles completed on time and cycles with issues.

Payment retries, automatic pause, late evidence, refunds, and failed service obligations still require policy decisions before implementation.

## 6. Finite and emergency campaigns

| Use case | Beneficiary | Completion condition | Primary evidence |
|---|---|---|---|
| Surgery / medicine | Verified patient or provider | The agreed invoice is paid | Facility invoice, acknowledgement, payment status |
| Stolen gig-driver motorbike | Verified driver | Replacement vehicle purchased/delivered | Invoice, handover, beneficiary confirmation |
| Family loses its income earner | Verified family | Defined support package/period completed | Transfers, primary needs, acknowledgement |
| Legal aid | Client or legal body | Funded case milestone completed | Redacted payment and milestone evidence |
| Disaster | Verified post/organization/community | Defined response phase completed | Logistics, safe distribution list, location, acknowledgement |
| Animal rescue | Verified shelter/vet/carer | Treatment or relocation completed | Vet invoice, progress, handover/adoption |

A campaign cannot call itself “complete” merely because money was transferred. The completion condition must be defined before publication.

## 7. Three creator–beneficiary relationships

### Creator helps a separate individual

Example: Ahwar helps an elderly vendor. The beneficiary wallet and Creator Reward are clearly separated.

### Creator helps a separate institution

Example: a creator raises for an elderly home. The home confirms receipt while the creator manages story, coordination, and evidence.

### Creator belongs to the beneficiary movement

Example: an environmental-movement member creates a campaign for the movement's own operations. The conflict of interest must be visible: project/organization wallet, signers, Creator Reward, and reviewer cannot be collapsed into one undisclosed party.

This third model is especially relevant to Pandawara-type movements and needs dedicated governance before the contract is finalized.

## 8. Why these use cases fit the initial market

- the work existed before Ulurin;
- outcomes can be defined and documented;
- the creator or institution has an initial community;
- funding needs are real and often recurring;
- fee transparency resolves an existing tension;
- prior campaign reputation can improve the next campaign's conversion.

Ulurin does not need to begin as “every charity for everyone.” A stronger wedge is creators and institutions already doing the work, carrying recurring needs, and willing to make outcomes inspectable.
